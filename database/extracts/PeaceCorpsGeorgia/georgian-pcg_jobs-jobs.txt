Here are a few jobs:

დირექტორი - Director
(direktori)

მენეჯერი - Manager
(menejeri)

მოხალისე - Volunteer
(moxalise)

In Georgian it is common for the verb to go last, but in general word order is flexible, so a word can be brought forwards in the sentence to emphasise its importance. The following two sentences both mean: "You are a director".

შენ დირექტორი ხარ.
შენ ხარ დირექტორი.

Georgian is also a 'pro-drop' language, so the pronoun can be dropped if meaning is preserved, thanks to verb agreement. The following three Georgian sentences therefore all mean "I am a volunteer".

მე მოხალისე ვარ.
მე ვარ მოხალისე.
მოხალისე ვარ.

For the third person form of 'to be' (She/he/it) it is also allowed to put an 'ა' at the end of the object of the sentence. This added 'ა' is called an enclitic. The following sentences both mean "She is a manager".

ის მენეჯერი არის
ის მენეჯერია
